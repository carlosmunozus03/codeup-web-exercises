Frequents Questions and Answers

How much does the entrance to a National Park cost?
Some protected areas have tariffed Access Rights (11 out of 46), including Bonus Rates. You can also check the entrance fees to each area by entering the tab you are interested in visiting and working there, in the upper right column, the Fees section.
How do you pay for access to a protected area? Do you accept credit card? Can I buy tickets through the website?
The payment of Access Rights in the corresponding protected areas (see more here)is made in cash. In the El Palmar, Iguazú, Lago Puelo, Los Arrayanes, Los Glaciares, Nahuel Huapi, Talampaya and Tierra del Fuego National Parks credit / debit cards are accepted (Visa, Mastercard and / or Américan Express, depending on the Park). In addition, you can buy your ticket online in advance:
Are there schedules to enter the Parks and Reserves?
Most of the protected areas have both entry and exit times, especially in the case of trails. It is important that you check this information before making your visit to a Park or Reserve.
Can I enter a protected area with my pet?
Pets are not allowed in National Parks and Reserves because they are exotic species that alter the environment and native fauna.
I visited a protected area and I want to make a suggestion / complaint. Who am I talking to?
On the page of each protected area you will find the direct contact of the National Park or Reserve so that you can directly send your suggestion.
I want to share my images with National Parks. Where do I send them?
Travelers who wish to share their images with National Parks so that they can later be published on the APN's social networks can send them through a private message to the agency's Facebook account. Of course, we will mention your details in the image credits when we publish it.
I want to volunteer in a protected area. As I do?
In the regulations section you can access the Resolution and the corresponding Annex of the National Volunteer Program for National Parks, as well as the application.
I would like to record a documentary in a National Park. What steps should I take?
For the realization of photographic and / or audiovisual productions, as well as for notes or press information, send an email detailing the reasons for your request to conafnap@email .com
I want to work in National Parks. Where do I send my resume?
Enter the Central Public Board of Public Job Offers, upload your personal data and upload your CV to be notified of future opportunities.
How do I become a National Park Ranger?
All the information related to the training of National Park Rangers is available at this link. For more information or particular inquiries, you can contact the Coordination of Training in Protecteddd Areas by email at rangerpark@emain.com or by phone at (333) 444 5555.
